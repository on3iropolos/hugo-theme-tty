# Learning in Public

*April 12, 2026*

## The Fear

"I'm not qualified to write about this. Someone will correct me. I'll look foolish."

This fear keeps us silent. It keeps us from sharing what we've learned. It's wrong.

## Why Share?

### You Learn Better
Explaining forces understanding. Writing about a concept reveals gaps in your knowledge. Teaching exposes what you don't know.

### Others Benefit
Your struggles are someone else's struggles. The bug that took you three days might save someone else three hours.

### Build Reputation
Writing builds credibility. It shows expertise, communication skills, and willingness to help.

### Career Opportunities
People hire writers. Technical writers get interviews. Authors get speaking slots. Visibility leads to opportunity.

## How to Start

1. **Write weekly** - Even short posts build the habit
2. **Document your mistakes** - Failures are content
3. **Share code** - Open source your learning projects
4. **Engage community** - Respond to others, join discussions

## The Imposter Syndrome

Everyone feels like an imposter. The senior engineer doubts themselves. The expert fears being exposed. Write anyway.

## Start Today

Write one blog post. Share one snippet. Post one tweet about what you learned. The habit starts with one action.
